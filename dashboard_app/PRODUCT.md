# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Mixed audience across three use cases:
- **Researchers & bioinformaticians**: exploring factor-level data, gene expression, and survival curves. Need density, precision, and the ability to drill into statistics.
- **Clinicians & collaborators**: reviewing key findings (Factor 5 protective, Factor 7 delayed protection, risk factors). Need clear takeaways with less statistical overhead.
- **Students & educational viewers**: learning about multi-omics analysis through the scrollytelling narrative and analysis flow pipeline.

Primary context: seated at a workstation, reviewing data in a research or educational setting. Not on mobile. Not in a rush.

## Product Purpose

Make multi-omics breast cancer research explorable. The dashboard translates 636 patients x 4 omics layers x 15 latent factors into navigable, interpretable visualizations. Success is when a viewer understands which molecular patterns drive survival in breast cancer and can explore the evidence themselves.

## Brand Personality

Calm, minimal, focused. The interface should feel like a well-designed research tool that stays out of the way. Let the data carry the weight. Confidence through restraint, not decoration.

## Anti-references

- **Warm AI defaults**: cream/sand backgrounds, soft rounded cards, the "safe" palette. This dashboard should feel clinical and precise, not cozy.
- **Bland admin templates**: generic Bootstrap dashboards with gray boxes and no personality. We need structure with intention, not boilerplate.
- **Overstimulating dashboards**: neon indicators, gradient everything, badges on badges. Data-dense does not mean visually loud.
- **Sci-fi/terminal aesthetic**: neon on black, glowing borders, matrix-aesthetic. Hard to read, style over substance.

## Design Principles

1. **Data-forward, always**: Every design choice should make data easier to read, not harder. If a visual element doesn't serve the data, remove it.
2. **Earned density**: Show as much information as the user needs, but never more. Dense where it matters (tables, plots), spacious where it aids comprehension (narrative sections).
3. **Dual-mode clarity**: Light and dark modes must be equally usable, not just color-inverted. Each mode should feel intentional.
4. **Consistent vocabulary**: Same component types, same interaction patterns, same visual language across all 7 pages. Predictability is a feature.
5. **Statistical honesty**: Visualizations should accurately represent the data. No truncated axes without disclosure, no misleading scales, no decorative chartjunk.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard accessibility: readable text sizes, sufficient contrast ratios (4.5:1 minimum for body text), logical tab order for keyboard navigation. Color is not the sole conveyor of meaning (patterns, labels, or icons supplement color-coded data). No specific WCAG level target beyond baseline readability.
